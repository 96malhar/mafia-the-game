Role guide

Town

Wins when every mafia-aligned player is gone.

  • Villager — no night action; relies on the day's discussion and vote to find the mafia.
  • Detective — each night, investigates one player and learns whether they're mafia. Can't check themselves — and a clean result isn't a guarantee: the Consort (and a Yakuza that has already recruited) read as "not mafia."
  • Doctor — each night, protects one player (including themselves); a protected target survives an attack that night. The save is silent — you only know it worked if your target lives.
  • Vigilante (optional) — has a single bullet for the whole game; on one night may shoot one player, or hold fire to keep the bullet for later. A shot the doctor saves still spends the bullet.

Mafia

Wins once they outnumber the town (or in a final one-on-one). Bare parity isn't enough — the town can still vote back.

  • Mafia — each night the mafia secretly agree on one player to kill (whoever locks a target first decides it). Immune to the Consort's distraction.
  • Consort (optional) — each night, distracts one player, blocking their action. Doesn't know the other mafia (and reads as "not mafia" to the Detective); wins with the mafia, and takes over the kill if the mafia are wiped out.
  • Yakuza (optional) — a mafia member who may instead, once per game, recruit a townsperson into the mafia — forgoing the kill that night and sacrificing themselves to do it. The sacrifice can't be prevented — not even by the doctor.
